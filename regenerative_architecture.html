Is it possible to define the life cycle of a building ?
Can disintegration give birth to something unique and wondeful ?
The project aims to rejuvenate the indigenous salt farming community of the great Rann in Gujarat, India, namely, the Agariyas, who are responsible for the production and supply of 76% of the salt in India and surrounding nations. The saltpan workers form a part of India’s most backward communities, with a major part of the community having no recognition, even in the Government registers, which sends out a silent yet strong plea for help.
This project hopes to become an architectural paragon which caters to the dreams of the region by using ‘salt’ itself as the basic building block of the community’s macrocosm. Since salt is the imperative component throughout their lives, the idea is to use it for the formation of foldable shelters, which will provide them with a retractable shelter, a necessity for them who lead the life of urban nomads. Bringing in an ecological renaissance, followed by a social renaissance, is the major focus by providing them with an ideal basic structure, born from region itself, reclaiming the identity of the community.
