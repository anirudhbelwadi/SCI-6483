The exquisite corpse was a game routinely used by Surrealists to “disrupt the waking mind’s penchant for order”. It hinged on free play and generated unexpected results “unimaginable by one brain alone”. We seek to continue this game today through the lens of machine learning algorithms paired with our collective agency as designers.
As part of the workshop, the final project undertaken by me was to explore the extent of abstract expressionism within architecture. The Abstract Expressionist philosophy was manifested in architecture in the form of Deconstructivism. Many deconstructivist architects designed buildings that themselves looked deconstructed, as if fragmented into sections. Others designed buildings that mimic the gestural aesthetics of Abstract Expressionist art. The finished visual appearance is characterized by unpredictability and controlled chaos. After the latent space and latent walk was generated from the StyleGAN training, 2D and 2.5D object manipulation studies were performed.

Abstract Expressionism: Art to Architecture with ML

Project developed as part of the J-Term course 'Exquisite Corpse 2.0' at Harvard GSD

Offered by: Dongyun Kim and George Guida

Underlying Principles:
Dataset scraping and augmentation
GAN based ML training
Latent walk, 2D and 2.5D object manipulation
